Appendix A — Assumptions & Phase History

Phase History

  • Phases 1–2: DEM fetch (OpenTopography fallback chain), slope cost, OSM buildings/water
  • Phase 3: D8 flow-accumulation stream fallback
  • Phase 4: Two-resolution coarse-to-fine routing, corridor band masking
  • Phase 5.0–5.4: Numba JIT, class-based road discounts, extended LULC table (18 categories), routing polish
  • Phase 6: Vertical alignment — grade-clipping FGL, parabolic VCs, AASHTO K-values, SSD check
  • Phase 7: Earthwork volumes — trapezoidal AEA, Brückner mass-haul, CSV export
  • Phase 8: Bridge & culvert inventory — Shapely crossing detection, D8 culvert siting, cost estimation
  • Phase 9: Parametric cost model — 9-component itemised estimate, LULC-weighted land acquisition
  • Phase 10: This automated feasibility report (Jinja2 → WeasyPrint)

Design Assumptions

  • DEM: SRTM 30 m — vertical accuracy ±5–16 m; horizontal accuracy ±20 m
  • OSM building/water/LULC data may be incomplete in remote areas (see data confidence flag)
  • Earthwork cross-sections are simplified trapezoids; geotechnical investigation required for detailed design
  • Bridge costs use World Bank ROCKS Myanmar mid-range (USD 3,500/m²); actual spans require hydrological study
  • Land acquisition rates from Myanmar DRD RAP guidelines 2022; subject to market fluctuation
  • Contingency (20%) reflects preliminary design maturity per ADB Category B guidance
  • All costs in USD; exchange rate fluctuation not modelled
